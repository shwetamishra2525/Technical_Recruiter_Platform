BSTRACT
ARTIV
 – Bid Online
 is an innovative online platform designed to empower small artists by providing them with a space to sell and buy artworks conveniently. The platform integrates advanced visualization technologies such as Augmented Reality (AR) view and 360-degree rotation, allowing buyers to see how an artwork will appear in their space before purchasing. 
It functions as both an art auction platform and a direct e-commerce marketplace, catering to diverse buyer preferences. Users can participate in bidding wars from the comfort of their homes or opt for direct purchases. By bridging the gap between artists and art enthusiasts, ARTIV fosters a thriving digital art marketplace that enhances accessibility, transparency, and engagement in the art industry. It redefines the online art-buying experience, making art more immersive and accessible.

CHAPTER 1: Introduction
1.1 Background
Art auctions have long been one of the most fascinating aspects of art collection, bringing together collectors, enthusiasts, and artists in a competitive yet engaging marketplace. Traditionally, auctions were considered an exclusive domain, characterized by high-profile events, glamorous guest lists, and secretive bidding wars. The process of acquiring art through auctions has always been thrilling, as buyers compete for unique pieces that hold both aesthetic and investment value. However, despite the allure of these events, traditional art auctions have remained largely inaccessible to many artists and buyers due to high entry barriers, geographical limitations, and the dominance of well-established auction houses.
With the rise of 
digitalization and the internet
, the art market has undergone a significant transformation. Online auction platforms have emerged, breaking the barriers of exclusivity and allowing a broader audience to participate in art trading. Unlike traditional auctions, online art marketplaces provide 
greater accessibility, transparency, and convenience
 for both artists and buyers. Digital platforms allow small and emerging artists to gain visibility, showcase their work to a global audience, and sell their art at fair market prices. Simultaneously, buyers benefit from a streamlined experience where they can explore and purchase artworks from the comfort of their homes.
In response to this evolving landscape, 
ARTIV
 is designed as an innovative 
online art auction and e-commerce platform
 that empowers artists and art collectors alike. ARTIV offers a 
dual marketplace model
, allowing users to either participate in 
auctions
 or purchase artworks 
directly
 through a traditional e-commerce system. Unlike conventional online marketplaces, ARTIV integrates 
advanced visualization features
 such as 
Augmented Reality (AR) view
 and 
360-degree rotation
, enabling buyers to preview how an artwork would look in their space and explore intricate details before making a purchase.
By combining the competitive nature of art auctions with the convenience of e-commerce, ARTIV aims to create an 
inclusive, engaging, and artist-friendly marketplace
. It provides emerging artists with a 
fair platform to showcase their talent
, ensures buyers have a seamless
 
purchasing experience, and fosters a thriving digital community where art is appreciated, valued, and easily accessible to all
.

ARTIV, being an online art auction platform, handles sensitive data including user credentials, payment information, and digital artwork content
. 
Firstly, weak authentication methods can lead to unauthorized access. Without strong password policies and multi-factor authentication, accounts become vulnerable to hacking and misuse. 
Data privacy is another critical concern. User
’
s personal information and transaction details need to be securely stored and encrypted, or they may be exposed to data breaches. Additionally, the auction mechanism is prone to manipulation—fake accounts or automated bots may place false bids or snipe auctions unfairly. Payment processing, if not integrated securely, can lead to financial fraud or loss of customer trust.
Moreover, the platform may be exposed to technical threats such as SQL injection and cross-site scripting (XSS) if user inputs are not properly validated. Insecure file uploads, especially with AR and 360° view features, can be exploited to upload malicious files.
